Cyber Attack

A cyber attack is an assault launched by cybercriminals using
one or more computers against a single or multiple computers or networks.

Major attack

Malware.
Ransomware.
Distributed denial of service (DDoS) attacks.
Spam and Phishing.
Corporate Account Takeover (CATO)

Malware

     A malware attack is a common cyberattack where malware (normally malicious software)
    executes unauthorized actions on the victim's system

Ransomware

    Ransomware is a malware designed to deny a user or organization access to files on their computer

Distributed denial of service (DDoS) attacks.

     DDoS Attack means "Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) Attack" and it is a cybercrime in which the attacker floods a server
         with internet traffic to prevent users from accessing connected online services and sites.

Spam and Phishing

Spam is unsolicited email, instant messages, or social media messages.

Corporate Account Takeover (CATO)

Corporate Account Takeover (CATO) occurs when cyber criminals gain access to your computer systems
         and steal your business online banking information.
